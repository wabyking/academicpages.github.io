Benyou Wang

Mail:   wabyking@163.com

Currently, I am a Marie Curie Researcher at University of Padova, Italy. With a good background of both academics and industry, I am aiming to develop a novel Quantum formalism for language understanding, which can also applicable to the high-level industrial application. I received my master degree in Tianjin University, and published many publications in top Conferences like SIGIR/AAAI/IJCAI/CIKM and Journals like Entropy/TCS/Information Sciences, in only three-year research. One of my co-authored papers won the SIGIR best paper honorable mention reward in 2017.

Then I worked as associate researcher in Tencent (Shenzhen) for nearly one year, and Research Consultant in Sougou (Beijing) for a short time, with industrial projects like customer service in the cloud, short-video stream recommendation and fast DL-based approach for input method.

Benyou's research interests include (but not limited) information retrieval, natural language processing, machine learning and quantum mechanics. He is now working on many textual problems, models, algorithms and innovations. Particularly,

  • Quantum probability driven neural network for texual represeantion and matching, in a more interpretable way.
  • Reconsidering machine learning (especially neural network) in a quantum perspetive, e.g. in high-dimention tensor space/hibert space.
  • Tasks in IR (e.g. Query Expansion, Recommendation System, Session Search) and NLP (e.g. Question Answering, Image caption).
  • Adversarial Learning, Reinforce Learning and Multi-task Learning
